What the Map Shows

This map shows the relationship between voting and the economic levels of each state. The colors of the states change from bright to dark and dark to bright when changing from the VEP highest office, VAP highest office and the percent in labor compared to the percent of non-citizens and the percent below the poverty line. The map shows a correlation between the amount of people who vote and amount of people who work and the amount of people below the poverty line.

Definitions

The VEP highest office is the percentage of the voting-eligible population that voted for highest office. VEP excludes people who are ineligible to vote. People who are ineligible to vote are non-citizens, felons and mentally incapacitated persons. The data also does not include people who are in the military or people who live overseas.

The VAP highest office is the percentage of the voting-age population that voted. The voting-age population consists of people who are 18 years or older according to the Bureau of the Census.

The percentage of noncitizens is the percentage of people in each state who are not citizens.

The percent in labor force refers to the percentage of people who work in each state.

The percent below the poverty line represents the population below the poverty line in each state in 2008.
